League of Legends V25.S1.1[edit | edit source]

Client[edit | edit source]

2025 Annual Cycle Thematic Seasons
  • Season 1: Welcome to Noxus
  • Annual release cycles will now be split into 3 Thematic Seasons, or simply Seasons, each lasting around 8 patch cycles (~4 months). Each Season may be themed around factions or alternate universes and centered around champion releases/updates.[1][2]
    • The theming can tie into game modes and meta-game experiences, rewards tracks, gameplay visuals, cosmetics, and all related content.
  • New 'Bringer of Ruin' in-Client motion comic. Available until January 23rd, 2025, 12:00 PST.
  • The Home page of the Client has been upgraded. Separate sections of interest are now organized into labeled tabs on the left. Includes tabs such as:
    • The current season's theme
    • Act Battle Pass
    • Other in-client experiences
    • Patch Notes
    • A new Info Hub area for showcasing news and highlights in or related to League of Legends and Teamfight Tactics.
    • And others.
Battle Pass
  • A Season is further divided into two Acts. Each Act has a corresponding Battle Pass which offers a unique rewards track and is always available.
    • Average free Battle Pass rewards include:
      • One themed Seasonal skin permanent.
      • One random 1350 or lower Skin Shard.
      • A unique title.
      • Two emotes.
      • Two Mystery emotes.
      • Three profile icons.
      • Two Mystery icons.
      • Four Champion Capsules.
      • 4750
      • 5 infinitely repeating "Epilogue" milestones at the end of the main reward track: the first four milestones are part of the free Pass and grant 50 per level ( 200 per loop).
    • Average paid Battle Pass rewards include:
      • Every free reward.
      • The Act's Prestige skin.
      • 3x Ancient Sparks.
      • Three Reward Skins.
      • Three Borders.
      • Seven Loot Orbs.
      • One Banner.
      • One Ward Skin.
      • Two Emotes.
      • Two Icons.
      • 1000
      • Infinitely repeating "Epilogue" milestones at the end of the main reward track: the last level is part of the paid Pass and grants 25 (once per loop).
  • The Battle Pass is accompanied by Daily, Weekly, and per-Act Objectives that feed into the Battle Pass track's progression.
  • Champion Mastery is now rewarded through the Battle Pass.
  • Removed: Event passes, event tokens & event shop.
    • Prestige skins and cosmetics are no longer tied to event passes.
    • One Prestige skin will be available each Act to earn as the ultimate paid Battle Pass reward.
  • Battle Pass Reward skins will not be added to the reroll pool.
  • Removed: Champion Capsules and Glorious Champion Capsules.
    • Rewards are incorporated into the Battle Pass track.
  • Removed: Players no longer gain a free 10 Mythic Essence every 50 levels. The currency is now exclusively premium.[3]
Champion Mastery[4]
  • Rewards are now incorporated into the Battle Pass track, earned via missions.
    • New: Missions for completing Mastery Milestones.
      1. (Indefinitely repeating) Complete 1 Mastery Milestone on any champion. Awards 100.
      2. (Once per season) Achieve Milestone IV on any champion. Awards 500.
    • Removed: Hextech Chests can no longer be earned from the Champion Mastery track.
      • Achieving Milestone I no longer rewards a Hextech Chest (up to 6 from this milestone per split)
      • Achieving Milestone III no longer rewards a Hextech Chest (up to 25 from this milestone per split)
    • Removed: Players can no longer earn a Superior Hextech Chest (by completing 12 Milestones in a Mastery Set).
  • Undocumented: Milestone requirement changes.
    • Milestone I
      • New requirement: 1 game of A- and higher
      • Old requirement: 1 game B- and higher, 4 games of C- and higher
    • Milestone II
      • New requirement: 1 game of A- and higher
      • Old requirement: 1 game A- and higher, 4 games of C- and higher
    • Milestone III
      • New requirement: 1 game of S- and higher
      • Old requirement: 1 game S- and higher, 4 games of C- and higher
    • Milestone IV
      • New requirement: 1 game of S- and higher
      • Old requirement: 1 game S- and higher, 4 games of C- and higher
    • Bonus Milestone
      • New requirement: 2 games of S- and higher
      • Old requirement: 3 games of S- and higher
  • Mastery Set rewards
    • New (Seasonal Missions):
      • At 12 Mastery Milestones completed 1500.
      • At 25 Mastery Milestones completed 500.
      • At 40 Mastery Milestones completed 500.
    • Old (separate Split Milestones track):
      • At 12 Mastery Milestones completed 1 Superior Mastery Chest, which is part of the same loot pool as a regular Masterwork Chest.
      • At 24 Mastery Milestones completed 750.
      • At 40 Mastery Milestones completed 1250.
Hextech Crafting
  • Removed: Players can no longer obtain Masterwork Chests in any way. (These would be granted via Ranked and Event rewards tracks, real-life promotions, as well as direct purchases from the Store as single or bundle items and as part of other bundles.)
  • This is an announcement for a future change. The Masterwork Chest progression track is expected to be retired with the release of patch 25.S1.5.
    • Rewards are now incorporated into the Battle Pass track.
  • Key Fragments are now crafted into the equivalent amount of Keys at once.
Honor System Rework
  • This is an announcement for a future change. The Honor Shop and Honor tokens are expected to be retired on patch 25.S1.4.
    • Players will receive future yearly Honor skins as well as all past Honor skins by acquiring and sustaining Honor level 5.
      • All existing Honor skins will also be distributed to all players at Honor level 5 after this update is released.
    • Unspent Honor 5 Tokens will be automatically converted to 1050 each.
Missions
  • Removed: First Win of the Day repeating mission that granted 400 XP and 50.
  • Removed: Missions no longer have a fallback of "participation" points (earned points for time spent playing). This has been effectively replaced by participation missions (e.g. "play a number of games") and/or other types of generic missions.
  • New: Daily, Weekly, per-Act missions for the Act's Battle Pass; Mastery, Event and other related missions. Rewards typically include Battle Pass XP and rarely icons and other non-premium content.
    • Progression and rewards from these missions are consolidated with the Battle Pass.
  • Added new Objectives UI window for the purposes of missions. [Location of the Missions button is unchanged.]
    • There are two menus, one for League of Legends missions and one for Teamfight Tactics missions (toggled from the logo buttons at the top left).
    • The left side displays the different categories as tabs.
    • The top side displays the current Pass, the player's current level and XP progress, and reward for reaching the next level.
    • The rest of the menu lists the player's current missions along with their description, progress and reward(s).
      • The menu also shows the remaining time to reset time-gated missions, the player's total active missions in a category, as well as their seasonal Mastery Set under the 'Mastery' category specifically.
Mythic Shop
  • This is an announcement for a future change. Starting in Patch 25.S1.2, Mythic Shop listings will be rotated every patch instead of every two patches. The current schedule for the Mythic Shop content in 25.S1.1 is unchanged.
Performance
  • Improved Client load times, especially on lower spec devices.
  • In the General tab of the Client settings, there is no longer an option to "Close League Client as Needed". Only the option to always close remains.
Practice Tool
  • Added access to multi-player, up to 10 players.
  • New cheats:
    • Set Game Speed to:
      • 0.25x
      • 0.5x
      • 1x
      • 2x
    • Spawn Ruinous Atakhan in Top Lane
    • Spawn Ruinous Atakhan in Bot Lane
    • Spawn Voracious Atakhan in Top Lane
    • Spawn Voracious Atakhan in Bot Lane
    • Complete Feats of Strength.
      • Can only be overridden by one team.
Queues
  • Swiftplay replaces Quickplay for Summoner's Rift.[5] It will initially launch in the following regions: NA, LAN, LAS, EUW, EUNE, RU, BR, TR, TW, VN. Swiftplay's launch will take place alongside the release of patch 25.S1.1.
    • In the lobby, players can select the same champion in two different roles, unless that champion is among the highest pick rate champions overall.
    • Sources of gold and experience grant more of each later in the game.
    • Increased effectiveness of comeback xp / gold.
    • Decreased effectiveness for taking down players who are behind.
    • Turret plates fall earlier.
    • Baron Nashor spawns earlier.
    • Teams only need 3 drakes to achieve soul.
    • Support and Jungle quests complete faster.
    • Nearby minions that die grant reduced gold if not killed by a champion.
    • Support items cost more but have increased stats.
  • Co-Op vs. AI queues are now using Swiftplay game rules.
Ranked[1]
  • 2025, Season 1 begins on 12:00:00 in every server according to local time.
  • Ranks and MMR will no longer reset after each Season (previously Split), but only after the launch of the new annual cycle.
    • With the beginning of Season 1, 2025, players will incur the one and only reset to their Rank standing for the entirety of 2025 (and so on for future annual cycles).
  • Removed: Ranked rewards track.
    • Rewards are now incorporated into the Battle Pass track.
    • Players now achieve eligibility for each season's Victorious skin by winning 15 Ranked games (in Solo/Duo and Flex combined).
  • Duo restrictions in Solo/Duo queue are lifted for the duration of this patch cycle, 25.S1.1. They will be re-enabled at the start of the next patch cycle.
Servers[6]
  • The following shards will be retired when the 14.24 patch cycle ends (January 9th, 3 AM local time):
    1. SG Singapore, Malaysia, & Indonesia (SG2)
    2. TH Thailand (TH2)
    3. PH The Philippines (PH2)
  • Their regions will be merged into a single Southeast Asia (SEA) shard, available alongside this patch's launch.
  • Accounts will be transferred automatically; players will not be required to perform any steps (a first-time login may be required).
Misc
  • A new "Version" tab has been added at the bottom of the Client settings that displays details about the game version, including the internal version (which uses the old scheme).

Game[edit | edit source]

Ambient game start
  • Reduced to 1:00 from 1:10 on Summoner's Rift.
    • Minion and Monster spawning is now offset 10 seconds later to keep the actual time of their first spawn unchanged.
Bloody Petals
  • New: This permanent, neutral buff is granted by picking up Blood Roses or Large Blood Roses, and as a direct reward from taking down Ruinous Atakhan.
  • Bloody Petals: For each stack, gain 25  experience and 0.6 0.2 (based on stacks) bonus Attack Damage or 1 0.333 (based on stacks) Ability Power (Adaptive). Each team member is granted an additional 0% 100% (based on their KDA)  experience.
  • Stacks infinitely.
Champion Kill Bounties
  • Removed: There is no longer a buffer amount of 50 gold when entering a negative bounty state.
  • Bounty accrual from kills and assists while in a negative state reduced to 1 bounty gold : 5 gold earned from 1 bounty gold : 4 gold earned.
  • Removed: First Blood no longer grants 100 bonus gold nor generates 50 bonus gold toward the assist bounty.
Feats of Strength - New Mechanic
  • A Best-of-Three (first to 2) competition to complete various "Feats". A teams earns 1 point upon:
    • An ally drawing First Blood
    • Scoring the First Turret
    • Taking down the first 3 Epic monsters
  • The winning team is awarded the Blessing of Noxus:
    • Automatically modifies Tier 2 Boots into a Triumphant version, granting them a small stat bonus varying by item.
    • Unlocks paid, Tier 3 upgrades for Boots items. At least 2 Legendary-tier items must be equipped before being able to buy them.
  • The fulfillment of Feats of Strength is indicated below the announcement overlay upon completing one related action.
  • The current status for both teams can also be viewed in the scoreboard.
Gold generation
  • Passive gold generation now starts at 1:40 into the game instead of 1:50.
Homeguard
  • New Effect: Homeguard is now always available from 0:20 game time, granting 75% bonus movement speed until 14:00 game time.
    • [Note: This is the same value granted at 14 minutes. There are no changes to Homeguard after 14 minutes.]
  • Homeguard duration reduced to 7 seconds from 8.
  • Removed: While Hand of Baron is active, no longer increases Homeguard's haste to 125% 200% (based on minutes) bonus movement speed.
  • Respawn Homeguard haste increased to 75% bonus movement speed from 66.67%.
  • Respawn Homeguard duration increased to 20 seconds from 8.
  • New Effect: Respawn Homeguard's buff icon now has a timer noting its exact duration on the status bar.
  • New Effect: Noted the debuff on the status bar when Homeguard is disabled due to entering combat.
Jungle plants
  • New: Blood Rose / Large Blood Rose.
    • Gloom: Blood Roses periodically spawn around slain champions' death location around the map. Each needs 30 seconds to grow.
    • Additionally, 8 normal and 8 Large Blood Roses spawn in Atakhan's quadrant once his pit is formed and over the period until his spawn.
    • After Atakhan spawns, the plants will stop spawning around the map.
    • Ruinous Atakhan specifically grows 6 normal Blood Roses and 6 Large Blood Roses upon being taken down.
    • Picking up a Blood Rose permanently grants 1 stack of the Bloody Petals buff to all team members, and picking up a Large Blood Rose grants 3.
Minimap
  • The following changes are undocumented.
  • Improved fidelity of all objective and point-of-interest icons.
  • Improved hourglass timer visual and color contrast.
  • Minimap turret icon has been changed to match the updated Turret icon visual seen on the scoreboard's objective tracker section.
  • New minimap icon for Atakhan. Each form has a unique icon.
Objective Votes
  • Bug Fix: UI for Dragon contesting vote no longer becomes invisible when disconnecting from and reconnecting to the game in the span of an active vote.
Scoreboard
  • The following changes are undocumented.
  • Added objective tracker for Atakhan.
  • Champion status section now has a background for each team's side, colored in the respective team colors.
  • Each champion's row is now visually separated by a faint line.
  • New Feats of Strength section that shows the completion of each applicable objective, under the respective team's side and colored in the respective team colors.
    • The winning team is signified by a 'Boot' icon in their color.
  • In the objective timers section:
    • Added Atakhan as a large section between Baron Nashor and Dragons. Each form of Atakhan has a unique icon.
    • Improved the color clarity, made the trim shinier, and bevelled the corners.
    • Updated the icons for Blue Sentinel, Red Brambleback and Baron Nashor (Baron now matches the icon on the objective tracker section).
  • New Scoreboard UI at the bottom will track each team's completed Feats.
Shopkeepers
  • Undocumented: Selection outline of each model is now visible for both sides' shopkeepers.

Monsters[edit | edit source]

Atakhan, Bringer of Ruin - New Epic Monster
  • Spawns at 20 minutes at the entrance of either top and bottom lane in the river. The location is determined by the side (half of the map) where the most kills and damage have occurred by 14 minutes. This is done via an internal "score", and the amount of score added is different between kills and damage.
  • At 14 minutes, terrain will appear on the respective side, marking his camp's location. The terrain consists of two walls in a circular arrangement, each facing each other along the river line. The river brush at the entrance where Atakhan spawns is removed.
  • Over the course of the next 6 minutes, 8 Blood Roses and 8 large Blood Roses will grow in the same quadrant of the map as the camp.
  • Can spawn in one of two forms, Voracious and Ruinous.
  • Both forms of Atakhan have identical basic attacks and a bolt ability, but also a unique special ability that differs between the two forms.
    • His attacks and abilities apply stacks of the Frail and Unworthy debuff for 4 seconds which may stack up to 50 times and refreshes with each attack and ability hit. For each stack, the target's resistances are reduced by 1, for a maximum of 50 resistances reduction.
      • Basic attacks apply 1 stack.
      • Bolt ability applies 5 stacks per bolt.
      • Form ability applies 3 stacks per hit.
  • Voracious Atakhan:
    • Spawns with lower score.
    • Ability deals damage over time in a 600-radius area under him.
    • Rewards all members of the slaying team with two buffs, a temporary and a permanent one.
    • False Life
      • Effect: Upon taking lethal damage, enter resurrection for 5.5 seconds, during which you are invulnerable, untargetable, and unable to act. After 2 seconds into the duration, you blink to your summoning platform. If taken to lethal damage by an enemy champion, they instead gain 1 stack of Bloody Petals and 100. The regular kill bounty is not dispensed, and on-kill/takedown effects do not trigger.
      • Duration: 150 seconds.
    • False Life
      • Effect: Gain an additional 40 when scoring a champion takedown.
      • Duration: Permanent.
  • Ruinous Atakhan:
    • Spawns with higher score.
    • Ability deals damage in 8 constricting rings over 2.5 seconds around his camp, starting at the circle outlined by the camp's terrain.
    • Upon being taken down, grants 6 stacks of Bloody Petals to the killing team, spawns 6 Blood Roses and 6 large Blood Roses around his camp and rewards all members of the slaying team with the Crown of Carnage buff.
    • Crown of Carnage
      • Effect: Gain 25% increased effects from all epic monster buffs.
      • Duration: Permanent.
      • Buff details:
        • Touch of the Void 25% more damage.
        • Hunger of the Void PENDING FOR TEST: Effect unknown.
        • Eye of the Herald Mercenary has 25% increased health, deals 25% increased damage to all targets, and duration of stun and knockup during Rift Herald Rodeo is increased by 25%.
        • Dragon Slayer Each stack's stat bonus is increased by 25%.
        • Chemtech Dragon Soul 25% more damage while below the threshold.
        • Cloud Dragon Soul 25% more bonus movement speed on both the regular and the super buff.
        • Hextech Dragon Soul 25% more damage and slow.
        • Infernal Dragon Soul 25% more damage.
        • Mountain Dragon Soul 25% larger shield.
        • Ocean Dragon Soul 25% more health and mana restoration.
        • Aspect of the Dragon 25% more damage.
        • Hand of Baron 25% more attack damage and ability power.

Turret

A turret, also called a tower, is a fortification that defends a part of territory. It provides vision of the area and autonomously fires homing bolts at nearby enemies, establishing a defensive ground for allies.

Turrets are most notable in Classic, defending against waves of enemy Minions from their ultimate goal of destroying the nexus. Champions work to destroy enemy turrets to push their assault into the enemies' territory. Turrets visually fall apart as they take damage.

Slow
A unit that is slowed has reduced  movement speed for the duration. Slow percentages cannot reduce a target below  110 movement speed. Some slows will instead modify the target's movement speed to a static value, which may not be further reduced nor increased.

Only the slow with the highest value is taken into movement speed calculations. All additional slows are ignored until the higher-valued slow wears off. The strength of slows may amplify or decay over their duration. Slows of the same debuff cannot stack and will instead refresh on each application unless otherwise stated.

Interrupts: Does not interrupt any actions.
Reduction: The duration is affected by Tenacity.

The strength is reduced by slow resist.

Resist: Resisted by crowd control immunity and slow immunity.
Removal: Removed by Cleanses, Garen's Decisive Strike, Master Yi's Highlander and Nunu's Biggest Snowball Ever!.

Shield
A shield adds hitpoints over the unit's  health that absorbs the next instances of damage in place of actual health. This can be improved by various items and effects depending on the type of shielding.

Incoming shields can trigger bonus effects from Shield Bash. Outgoing shields can trigger bonus effects from Summon Aery, Ardent Censer, and Staff of Flowing Water. Shield reduction effects, exclusively Serpent's Fang, reduce all incoming and outgoing shields.

League of Legends content
Heal
Healing replenishes a unit's  current health, but never above their maximum health.

Incoming heals can trigger bonus effects from Overheal. Outgoing heals can trigger bonus effects from Summon Aery, Ardent Censer, and Staff of Flowing Water.

Melee

Melee champions have a basic attack  range usually within 200 units. A melee attack does not use a projectile to deal damage, therefore not subject to effects that mitigate projectiles, like Wind Wall. Some champions can shift between a ranged and melee form, and certain items/runes differ for melee characters.

Ranged

Ranged champions have a basic attack  range usually beyond 300 units. Many deal damage through projectiles, which are subject to mitigating effects like Wind Wall. Some champions can shift between a ranged and melee form, and certain items/runes differ for ranged characters.

Death
Death occurs when a unit's  health is reduced to zero, thereby being killed. The unit can no longer act and is removed or becomes a corpse.

On player death, they can view the death recap and the shop to purchase items, and some item actives can be used while dead.
